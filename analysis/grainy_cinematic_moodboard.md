# Taste Analysis — "Grainy Cinematic Moodboard"

**Source:** Pinterest search `grainy cinematic moodboard aesthetic`
**Sample size:** 30 pins downloaded → `images/grainy_cinematic/`
**Analyzed:** 10 representative images viewed directly

---

## What's actually showing up across the set

Looking at the real frames (not Pinterest tags), the consistent visual ingredients are:

1. **Heavy film grain** — even on shots that were clearly captured digitally. Sometimes pushed into outright camcorder/VHS noise (see #18, the backlit window portrait).
2. **Intentional motion blur** — bodies in mid-stride (#11, running figure at golden hour), heads turning so the face streaks (#2, red-wall portrait), trees smeared past a moving car window (#1).
3. **Frame-within-a-frame** — car windows, rear-view mirrors, side mirrors, windowpanes, doorways. The viewer is always *looking through* something rather than at it directly.
4. **Color grading is dominant, never neutral.** Every image is pushed hard into one of a handful of palettes:
   - **Teal + orange** (#5 night convenience store, #30 car interior at sunset)
   - **Deep monochrome red/burgundy** (#2 portrait, the red gel washing the subject in #23)
   - **Sodium-yellow + black shadow** (#15 traffic light against orange wall — pure Wong Kar-wai)
   - **Faded warm pink/cream** (#26 suburban street at dusk)
   - **Cyan-green dusk** (#8 powerline silhouette with overlay text)
5. **Backlit silhouettes & blown-out highlights** — the sun or a window is allowed to clip to pure white. No HDR recovery.
6. **Solitary figures, faces hidden or turned away.** When a face is visible it's blurred or partly shadowed. The vibe is "you, but a stranger" — not portrait, but *avatar*.
7. **Lo-fi resolution is the point.** Several pins are visibly low-res or compressed, and that's read as authenticity, not failure.
8. **Square / 4:5 / 9:16 crops.** Phone-ready vertical or near-square — built for IG/Xiaohongshu carousel.
9. **East Asian visual vocabulary leaks in** — Japanese signage in #15, the 小红书 watermark in #23, narrow streets reading as Tokyo/Seoul/Shanghai.

---

## The "taste" in three sentences

You're drawn to **photographs that look like memories of a film you watched once and can't quite remember.** The grain, motion blur, and color cast all do the same job: they pull the image out of the present tense and into something more like recollection. It's less "moodboard" than "screencap from your own life if it had a colorist."

---

## The named aesthetic clusters this overlaps with

- **Wong Kar-wai / Christopher Doyle** lighting language (sodium yellows, red rooms, neon-on-skin)
- **Khaite / The Row campaign photography** (motion blur portraits, leather, red walls)
- **Xiaohongshu / 小红书 grainy filter trend** (cool blue with red-lit face)
- **35mm Cinestill / Portra 400** color science applied digitally
- **"Liminal cozy" / lonelycore / late-night drift** — convenience stores, empty streets, car interiors
- **Andrei Tarkovsky / Hou Hsiao-hsien** — the long, quiet, ambient shot

## Cross-reference with your earlier pin (the orb / dreamcore one)

The two tastes are **related but not the same**. Shared DNA:
- Solitary figures, often back-turned
- Soft focus / blur as emotional grammar
- Warm-cool color contrast
- One bright light source dominating the frame

Diverged:
- The earlier pin is **surreal / fantastical** (the orb, the prismatic refraction). This batch is **realist but stylized** — the world is still our world, just color-graded.
- The earlier pin reads digital / 3D / AI-render. This batch reads photographed / filmed.

**If we're building a single taste profile:** the unifying thread is *quiet solitary figures + heavy stylization of light*. Surrealism is one branch, grainy cinematic realism is another.
