## What's actually in the frame

A lone figure in a white shirt sits on a small folding chair (one warm orange/red flash on the chair back) atop a grassy ridge, reading. Behind them: a deep teal sky with a single bright sun/lens flare high and to the left. The whole scene is wrapped inside a giant **glass-orb / soap-bubble distortion** — you can see the curvature of the sphere catching highlights along its rim. The foreground grass dissolves into **prismatic, glittery bokeh** — refracted light scattering in chromatic specks across the bottom third. Outside the orb: black, void-like vignette.

It's a still that feels closer to a Hayao Miyazaki frame run through a macro lens than a photograph.

## The underlying "tastes" this signals

1. **Worlds-in-miniature.** You're drawn to images where the subject is *enclosed* — orbs, snow globes, dioramas, bubbles. The container is half the point.
2. **Solitude as cozy, not lonely.** A single figure with their back to camera, doing something quiet (reading). No drama, no faces.
3. **Warm-cool split.** Teal sky vs golden ground. This is the same color contrast that powers Studio Ghibli stills, sunset cinematography, and most "aesthetic" Pinterest boards.
4. **Light as texture.** Bokeh, prismatic refraction, lens flare — light itself is treated as a material, not just illumination.
5. **Soft over sharp.** Nothing in the frame is in crisp focus. Taste leans painterly / dreamy rather than documentary / hyperreal.
6. **One pop of saturation.** The orange chair-back is doing a lot of work — anchoring the eye in an otherwise blue-green palette.
